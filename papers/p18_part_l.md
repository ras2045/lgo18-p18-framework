The prime sits at a large lemniscate, event horizons far apart.

## 50.8 The Three Composite Signals

### Signal 1: Parity Sum → Lemniscate Apex (exact, algebraic proof)

```

```

**Algebraic proof:**
For prime P (odd) with even gap g:
- Even composites in (P, P+g): positions 1,3,...,g−1 from P (g//2 terms)
- Odd composites in (P, P+g):  positions 2,4,...,g−2 from P (g//2−1 terms)

```
diff = odd_sum − even_sum
     = sum(P+even_pos) − sum(P+odd_pos)
     = (−1)×P + (g//2−1)×(g//2) − (g//2)²
     = −P − g//2
     = −(P + g//2)   Q.E.D.
```

**Verified: 17,628/17,628 = 100%.**

negative of the prime lemniscate's right lobe apex. The composites
